# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences treated as one, undifferentiated: (1) Kenyan/African SMB owners evaluating whether to hire Brian for e-commerce, AI, or security work, similar to his existing case-study clients (Lirason, Panama, Maahir), and (2) international remote employers/recruiters screening him as a full-stack/AI engineering candidate against other portfolios. Neither audience gets a distinct path or voice; the site reads as credible to both at once.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that proves Brian Munyasia Musanga ships real, revenue-generating software, uses AI with cost discipline rather than novelty, and builds securely by default. Success is a visitor (client or employer) coming away confident enough to reach out via /contact.

## Brand Personality

Editorial and restrained, not startup-SaaS. Confident, precise, magazine-like use of type and whitespace over decoration. Technical authority matters more than visual trend-chasing — the site should read like someone who understands systems deeply (security, AI cost-engineering), not someone chasing this year's design fashion. No named reference site: the design should be unique to Brian, built out of the already-established heritage-luxury inversion system (butter/green, Instrument Serif display, General Sans body, JetBrains Mono details) rather than converging on a generic template. The hero should include Brian himself — a photo section (placeholder image for now) — so the site feels personal, not templated.

## Anti-references

Generic startup-SaaS scaffolding: hero-metric blocks, gradient text, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows on every section, identical icon-grid cards, side-stripe accent borders, glassmorphism-as-decoration. Nothing that could be mistaken for a template with the name swapped in.

## Design Principles

- Editorial confidence over SaaS convention — type and whitespace carry hierarchy, not cards or icons.
- Proof over claims — every section should point at a real shipped outcome (revenue, cost cut, security posture), not adjectives.
- One inversion system, used consistently — butter/green light-dark inversion, Instrument Serif for editorial emphasis, JetBrains Mono for structural/metadata detail, never diluted with a fourth typeface or off-system color.
- Personal, not templated — the site should be identifiably Brian's, including his own image, not swappable with another developer's name.
- Cost-aware, security-first framing carries through the copy and the craft — the site itself should feel deliberate and unbloated, matching the story it tells about his engineering.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast), honor `prefers-reduced-motion` on all animation, mobile-first responsive from 375px up, semantic HTML throughout. (Carried from the user's global engineering standards, not newly scoped here.)
